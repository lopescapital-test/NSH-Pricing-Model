§1 · The Three Tiers

How much do you want us walking you through it?

The tier ladder is built on one parent-comprehensible question: how much guided support do you want during the 12-week intensive? The protocol is the same. The kit is the same. What changes is how much we're with you while you execute it.

The three philosophies in one line each

Foundation: "We'll teach you, then you run with it."

Heavy front-load in Week 1. Weekly clinical check-ins after. Most of the protocol execution happens solo with periodic coaching. Best for families who learn fast and want autonomy.

Accelerated: "A healthy mix of guided and at-home work."

Multiple weekly clinical and concierge touchpoints during active phase. Mixed model — clinician adjusts, concierge coaches, parent executes the rest. The recommended path for most families.

Immersive: "We'll be with you every day, walking you through it."

Daily M-F concierge sessions during the active phase. Twice-weekly clinician sessions. Bi-weekly Kyle involvement. Maximum guided support for the most complex cases or families who want premium clinical density.

Why this framing works. Parents don't want to be told their child is a "Profile B HIGH-intensity case." They want to know how much hand-holding they're getting. The internal profile-tier matrix (§7) drives the concierge's recommendation; the family chooses based on philosophy alignment and budget capacity.

§7 · Internal Logic

Profile × Tier matrix

The clinical profile and intensity drive the recommended tier. This matrix is internal — used by the concierge during the Discovery Call to guide tier recommendations, not published in the brochure. Families choose their tier from the three options; the concierge guides them based on this logic.

Down-tier and up-tier policies

Down-tier (family chooses below recommendation). Allowed with documented risk. Concierge captures the down-tier reason (budget, capacity, family preference) in the patient file. Mid-program, if outcomes stall, the upsell conversation is pre-loaded ("we noted this risk at intake — let's adjust"). Profile B/E choosing Foundation requires explicit MD review.
Up-tier (family chooses above recommendation). Always allowed, no friction. A Profile D family who wants the daily-walk-through Immersive experience can have it — that's a service decision, not a clinical one. Up-tier sales generate higher LTV through stronger Continuing Care conversion. Encourage when family signals interest.
Refusal cases. Profile B or E families choosing LIGHT intensity / Foundation tier. The protocol load doesn't match the clinical complexity at any reasonable execution. Concierge offers Accelerated or Immersive; if neither works, refers out or defers enrollment.
§8 · The Sales Flow

How the Discovery Call uses this

The pricing model only works if the concierge can use it in real time on a 30-45 minute Discovery Call. Five steps. Profile in. Capacity in. Tier out. Family chooses. Risk documented if needed.

The five-step flow

1. Identify the profile

Concierge probes case history per the Discovery SOP §1-2. Output: one of A / B / C / D / E / F. This step is roughly 10 minutes of the call.

2. Identify daily capacity

"How much time can you commit per day to your child's homecare protocol?" Output: LIGHT (20-35 min) / MID (40-60 min) / HIGH (60-90 min). Roughly 5 minutes.

3. Look up the recommendation

Concierge consults the §7 matrix internally. The intersection of profile and capacity yields a recommended tier. This is mechanical — no judgment call.

4. Present the three tiers consultatively

"Based on what you've described, we'd recommend [Tier X]. Here's what that looks like. If you'd prefer more daily walking-through-it time, [Tier above] might fit better — that's about how much guided support you want, not because your child clinically needs it."

5. Family chooses; document any risk

If the family selects below the recommended tier, concierge captures the rationale and flags it for clinical review. If above, no friction — encourage the upgrade.

What the concierge does NOT say. The internal matrix uses Profile A/B/C/D/E/F language. Families never see those labels. The brochure and Discovery Call both stay parent-comprehensible: the three tiers are described as philosophies (teach-and-go / mixed / daily walking through), not as clinical assignments. The profile system is the engine; the philosophy framing is the interface.
