§4 · The 8 Weeks

Day one to graduation.

Phases are readiness-based, not week-locked. The arc below is the modal Foundational MID family — Sensory-Motor and lighter cases tend to step down faster.

WEEK 1
Onboarding
Kit lands Day 1. Concierge runs extended sessions (~90 min/day) for the first 3 days teaching every device. Baseline Mendi logged. Kyle confirms classification.
WEEKS 2-3
Protocol lock-in
Concierge settles to the standard 2-hr afternoon block. Mom knows the kit. Weekly OT/PT call. Reflex work begins to feel boring and repetitive — that's the point.
WEEKS 4-5
First wins
Most Foundational kids show first reflex resolution. Mendi delta usually visible. Concierge pulls in advanced Hub games (Sea Adventure) and tightens reflex progressions.
WEEKS 6-7
Depth
Labs return. OT/PT and Kyle review as parting recommendations — what to keep doing for gut-brain axis post-program. Concierge may drop presence to first 30 min.
Week 8 · Graduation
last week
Final Mendi scan. Wk-1 → Wk-8 delta presented to family with explanation of what changed. Exit documentation — what worked, what to maintain, what to revisit. Continuing Care offered separately. Mom is running this in 30 minutes on autopilot. Family decides whether to schedule a follow-up intensive.
§5 · Who This Is For

Two patterns. No exceptions.

The home intensive works for Foundational Developmental Delay (severity 2/10) and Sensory-Motor Disconnection (severity 3/10). Borderline cases default in. Strong signals of an underlying driver — immune, metabolic, structural, regulatory — route to treatment first.

Yes — home intensive

  • Foundational Developmental Delay pattern
  • Sensory-Motor Disconnection pattern
  • Family can commit to a consistent after-school 2-hr window, Mon-Fri, for 8 weeks
  • One parent willing to be the on-the-ground therapist
  • Reliable home internet for daily telehealth

Not yet — treatment first

  • Hyperimmune / PANS-PANDAS
  • Environmental Neuroinflammatory
  • Metabolic / Mitochondrial
  • Birth Trauma / Structural
  • Acquired Regression
  • Severe Neurodevelopmental Dysregulation
  • Foundational/Sensory-Motor kids not yet able to sustain a 2-hr block
"Not yet" doesn't mean turned away. Families in the right column receive clinical care to address the underlying driver — immune, metabolic, structural, regulatory — and to build the regulation and sit-tolerance the home intensive demands. The intensive is the next phase once the child is in the green. The pathway is not yet fully defined and needs a clinical and pricing design pass.
The afternoon-window question is the real filter. Two hours per day, five days a week, for eight weeks is a meaningful commitment. Families with packed school-activity schedules, two working parents with no caregiver overlap, or unstable home routines should be steered to treatment-first care and onboarded into the intensive later. The phone consult script needs to set this expectation before the family sees the price.
