Three pathways · clinical fit drives the route

Three intensives. One system.

NeuroHome offers three home neurological intensive pathways. Two are 8-week home intensives at different price points and concierge intensities (Plan A Full and Plan B Lite). The third — Pre-Intensive — is a readiness pathway for kids who can't yet sustain a 2-hr daily block, or whose underlying drivers need to be identified through labs first. The decision tree below determines clinical fit; the cards summarize each plan; the internal band underneath shows unit economics.

§ The 8 weeks

Day one to graduation

Phases are readiness-based, not week-locked. The arc below is the modal Foundational MID family — Sensory-Motor and lighter cases tend to step down faster.

WEEK 1
Onboarding
Kit lands Day 1. Concierge runs extended sessions (~90 min/day) for the first 3 days teaching every device. Baseline Mendi logged. MD reviews labs and confirms classification.
WEEKS 2-3
Protocol lock-in
Concierge settles to the standard 2-hr afternoon block. Mom knows the kit. Weekly OT/PT call. Reflex work begins to feel boring and repetitive — that's the point.
WEEKS 4-5
First wins
Most Foundational kids show first reflex resolution. Mendi delta usually visible. Concierge pulls in advanced Hub games and tightens reflex progressions.
WEEKS 6-7
Depth
OT/PT and MD review labs returning insights. Parting recommendations — what to keep doing for gut-brain axis post-program. Concierge may drop presence to first 30 min.
Week 8 · Graduation
last week
Final Mendi scan. Wk-1 → Wk-8 delta presented to family with explanation of what changed. Exit documentation — what worked, what to maintain, what to revisit. Continuing Care offered separately. Mom is running this in 30 minutes on autopilot. Family decides whether to schedule a follow-up intensive.

§ Who this is for

The premium-care pathway

Yes — Plan A Full

  • Foundational or Sensory-Motor profile
  • Child can sustain a 2-hr daily afternoon block
  • Family wants daily concierge presence and live MD/OT/PT
  • One parent available afternoons M-F for 8 weeks
  • Budget capacity for premium tier

Consider Plan B or Pre-Intensive instead

  • Budget-sensitive — consider Plan B Lite
  • Capable parent with strong learning curve — Plan B
  • Child can't yet sustain 2-hr blocks — Pre-Intensive
  • Complex underlying drivers — Pre-Intensive (labs-led)

§ Graduation credit

Earning the upgrade

Families who graduate from Pre-Intensive into Plan A or Plan B apply an escalating credit to the destination sticker. The credit grows with continued investment — families who needed longer continuation get larger credits at graduation, partly recovering what they invested in getting there.

§ The 8 weeks · entry phase

Entry phase arc

The entry phase has one job: figure out what the child needs, and start building regulation. Phase pacing is readiness-gated.

WEEKS 1-2
Onboarding + labs
Kit lands Day 1. Concierge 3×/wk teaching sensory anchors. Labs ordered Wk 1, samples mailed back. OT/PT initial 30-min protocol design call after kit lands.
WEEKS 3-6
Labs return + protocol
Concierge 2×/wk. Labs return Wk 4. MD 30-min live labs review with family — direction set. Protocol adjusts to lab findings (mold? gut? mito?). Mom learns the regulation rhythm.
WEEKS 7-8
Readiness assessment
Concierge 3×/wk. OT/PT end-of-entry assessment (30 min). Three possible outcomes: ready to graduate to Plan A/B, continue monthly until ready, or (rare) refer out for acute medical care if the child has moved into crisis.
END OF WK 8
Decision point
Family hits one of three forks: graduate up with credit, enter continuation at $500/mo, or refer out. Clinical decision, not sales decision.

§ Who this is for

The readiness pathway

Yes — Pre-Intensive

  • Any child not immediately ready for the Home Intensive
  • Dysregulated Foundational or Sensory-Motor profiles
  • Hyperimmune / PANS-PANDAS profiles
  • Metabolic / Mitochondrial profiles
  • Birth Trauma / Structural profiles
  • Acquired Regression profiles
  • Severe Neurodevelopmental Dysregulation
  • Family willing to commit to labs-led diagnostic phase plus monthly continuation until ready

Not Pre-Intensive

  • Child can already sustain a 2-hr daily block consistently — direct entry to Plan A or Plan B
  • Active medical crisis requiring acute hospital-level care — phone consult declines and refers out
The narrow out-of-scope cohort. With Pre-Intensive now serving all profiles that need diagnostic and regulation work before the Home Intensive, the truly out-of-scope cohort narrows to families in active medical crisis — kids who need acute care (hospital admission, IV treatments, surgical intervention) before any rehab pathway makes sense. Phone consult identifies these edge cases and declines, with referral language: "Your child needs acute medical care first. Reach back when stable and we'll start with Pre-Intensive."
